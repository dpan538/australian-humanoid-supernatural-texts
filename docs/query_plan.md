# Query Plan

Query rows are generated by `scripts/build_queries.py` from `config/queries.yml`, `config/lexicon.yml`, and `config/noise_rules.yml`.

## Date Bands

- `1803-1841`: retrospective Trove backsearch and negative-control window.
- `1842-1875`: early anchor period.
- `1876-1969`: pre-modern newspaper and publication expansion.
- `1970-present`: modern Yowie, heritage, tourism, and media period.
- `2004-present`: Google Trends attention series.
- `2015-07-01-present`: Wikimedia Pageviews attention series.

## Query Types

Exact phrase queries use quoted aliases such as `"Yowie"` or `"Quinkan"`.

Constrained boolean queries are required for high-noise terms. They combine a noisy term with contextual constraints such as `Aboriginal`, `Australia`, `spirit`, `Yowie`, or more specific variants.

Fuzzy variant queries are reserved for later manual expansion. V1 avoids automatic fuzzy searching because it can generate too much noise.

Negative-control queries are used in the 1803-1841 retrospective backsearch window and for control terms. They help detect anachronism, OCR artifacts, or unrelated term histories.

Attention-series terms are generated for Google Trends and Wikimedia Pageviews planning. These are not semantic corpus records.

## High-Noise Terms

The following bare queries are forbidden:

- `Yahoo`
- `Mimi`
- `Mamu`
- `Hairy Man`

These terms may appear only in constrained queries or as part of more specific phrases such as `"Yahoo-devil-devil"` or `"Hairy Man of the Wood"`.

## Validation Queue

Validation-queue terms are seeded into the lexicon with `validate_before_include`, but the V1 query builder does not generate normal collection queries for them. They require manual source checking and promotion before inclusion.

## Public Seed Collection

`scripts/collect_public_round.py` performs a deliberately small public-only seed collection round. It does not replace the query plan. Its purpose is to test the infrastructure, identify early or additional source leads, and create records that exercise review, ethics, and location workflows.

Trove/NLA items collected without API-key/full-page access are marked as public metadata leads. They should not be treated as full-text semantic evidence until a researcher verifies the article text.
