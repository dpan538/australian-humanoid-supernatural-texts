# Collection 3000 Strict Geography Plan

This plan governs the next expansion stage for the Australian Public Text Archive of Supernatural Humanoid Narratives and Encounters.

The target is **3,000 net-new accepted public source items with strict geographic evidence**. A source item without a verified coordinate does not count toward this target, even if it remains useful as a lead, context item, or card-only archive record.

## Counting Rule

An item counts only when it has all of the following:

- stable public URL or persistent identifier;
- title;
- publication or organisation;
- publication date or explicit date note;
- access date;
- source type and source tier;
- publicness and rights/access note;
- substantive evidence summary or short permitted excerpt;
- narrative type or secondary role;
- Australian relationship;
- humanoid basis;
- source label where present;
- location role;
- location text as printed;
- latitude and longitude;
- geocode source;
- geocode verification status;
- coordinate evidence note;
- ethics and cultural sensitivity status;
- duplicate check status.

## Strict Geography Gate

Accepted map-grade records must have a verified coordinate attached to a named place, locality, town, named feature, or exact site.

The following do **not** count as strict map-grade geography:

- country-only records;
- state or territory-only records;
- broad regions such as “outback,” “northern Australia,” or “Cape York” without a named locality;
- publication location alone;
- source institution location alone;
- inferred coordinates not supported by the source or a documented public gazetteer;
- decorative or algorithmic point placement.

These records may still remain in the archive as narrative records, leads, or review items, but they must not render as individual map flags.

## Quality Classes

### A. strict_primary_geocoded

Counts toward the 3,000 target.

Original or near-primary public source with a named Australian locality and verified coordinates. Examples include Trove article pages with publication details, digitized public-domain books, state library items, local history publications, and government or archival sources.

### B. strict_institutional_geocoded

Counts toward the 3,000 target.

Public institutional, library, museum, heritage, park, council, or community-controlled public item with substantive relevant content and verified locality coordinates.

### C. strict_reputable_retelling_geocoded

Counts toward the 3,000 target.

Reputable media, public broadcasting, documentary, or local-history retelling with a stable source chain, substantive narrative content, and verified coordinates.

### D. review_lead_geography_missing

Does not count toward the 3,000 target.

Relevant public item where the place is broad, missing, ambiguous, not yet geocoded, or only available as publication location.

### E. excluded_or_control

Does not count toward the 3,000 target.

Duplicate, control, non-humanoid, inaccessible, restricted, metadata-only, insufficient evidence, or ethically excluded material.

## Geographic Priorities

The first expansion pass prioritises under-covered or currently weakly mapped areas:

- Western Australia;
- South Australia;
- Northern Territory;
- Tasmania;
- Australian Capital Territory;
- Victoria;
- Queensland;
- New South Wales.

This is a collection priority, not a claim about the real distribution of narratives.

## Temporal Coverage

The 3,000 target should attempt coverage across:

- before 1842;
- 1842-1875;
- 1876-1918;
- 1919-1945;
- 1946-1969;
- 1970-1999;
- 2000-present.

Traditional narratives, spirit-person narratives, local legends, and rumours may use earliest public attestation or circulation period rather than event date.

## Source Policy

Preferred sources:

- Trove article pages with article IDs and publication details;
- National Library of Australia digitized items;
- state library digitized collections;
- Internet Archive public-domain books and periodicals;
- public newspapers, magazines, memoirs, local histories, and government reports;
- AIATSIS public catalogue guidance and community-controlled public resources;
- museums, galleries, parks, heritage authorities, and councils;
- ABC, SBS, and reputable journalism.

Discovery-only sources:

- AYR;
- Wikipedia;
- tourism pages;
- paranormal directories;
- blogs and forums.

Discovery-only sources can point to stronger sources, but they should not dominate accepted counts.

## Frontend Implication

Every accepted strict-geography record can render as one independent map flag. The flag must be projected from its latitude and longitude, not placed through artificial state packing.

Records that lack strict geography remain available for review, source lists, narrative analysis, and card-only export, but they must not be rendered as map points.
