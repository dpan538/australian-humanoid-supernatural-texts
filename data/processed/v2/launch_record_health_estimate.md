## Interpretation

The current corpus is visually usable but not yet research-healthy enough for a confident public launch. It is card-ready in the narrow frontend sense, but still has weak spatial precision, AYR/Yowie dependence, and many legacy records whose date is embedded in title text rather than normalized date fields.

## Recommended Launch Thresholds

### Internal prototype

1,000 to 1,500 records is enough only for private iteration when the goal is testing the visual system, record cards, map density, and source-policy workflow.

### First public research launch

2,000 to 3,000 normalized, accepted source items is the practical launch standard for this project.

Minimum health expectations:

- at least 1,500 non-AYR source items;
- at least 700 primary or near-primary items;
- all eight states and territories represented by accepted records;
- at least 30 percent locality-level or better location evidence;
- no single private aggregator above 25 percent of accepted public records;
- all records have source title, public URL or identifier, source organisation, date note, narrative type, source label, publicness status, and card body text;
- no restricted or culturally unsafe content in public export.

### Mature public archive

5,000 to 7,500 accepted source items is the stronger target if the archive is meant to support robust temporal, regional, and narrative-type comparisons.

### 10,000+ records

10,000 records is not necessary for launch and may reduce quality unless collection routes are already diversified and normalized. It should be treated as a long-term scale target, not a launch requirement.

## Current Status Against Standard

The current 985 records are useful for frontend validation but should be treated as a legacy-heavy baseline. The next data milestone should prioritize 500 to 1,000 high-quality non-AYR additions with normalized dates, location roles, and source relationships before chasing a larger headline number.
