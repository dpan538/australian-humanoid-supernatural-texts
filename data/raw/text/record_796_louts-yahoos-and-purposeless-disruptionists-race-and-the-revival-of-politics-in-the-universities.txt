The same could have been said specifically about the conscience of students. With a reputation for apathy students seemed rarely stirred at all; however, in response to events in Sharpeville, Sydney University saw a demonstration so loud that two o'clock classes were cancelled. Such enthusiasm was significant. According to Peter Coleman, writing in The Bulletin, it represented the 'revival of politics' at Sydney University. The Sydney case was not an isolated one.
